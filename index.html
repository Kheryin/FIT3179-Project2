Australian Livestock Exports

Australia's Livestock Export Trade is a valuable Australian industry as it is a major source of employment for many people in
rural and regional Australia. It is one of the country's largest value-adding and key economic industry which develops
national economic growth.

This visualisation aims to explore the patterns in the Australian Livestock Exports including its main destination countries
and species exported from years 2016 to 2020.


Overview Of Livestock Exports

The Australian livestock export sector is one of the largest worldwide in terms of annual volumes of livestock exported.

During the years 2016 to 2020, most of Australia's livestock are exported to the South East Asia region, and mainly to Indonesia.

From 2016 to 2020, the quantity of livestock exports seems to be decreasing generally. Particularly, the quantity of livestock exports seems to be the lowest during the months July to October for years 2018, 2019 and 2020.

From 2016 to 2020, the Cattle and Sheep species dominates the Australian livestock exports. The exported quantities of the Others species category in the line chart, which is the sum of Alpacas, Buffalo, Camels, Deer and Goats species exported, is even collectively lesser than that of the Cattle and Sheep species individually.

There seems to be a steady trend in the quantity of Cattles exported across time, whilist the number of Sheeps exported is noticably lesser during the July to October period for years 2018 to 2020. The Cattle species is mainly exported to Indonesia and China whilist the Sheep species is mainly exported to Kuwait, Qatar and Jordan. Other species are only exported to a few countries in small quantities.
